What is a Biome?

  • A biome is a region with a distinct climate and life forms
  • Includes natural (forests, deserts) and urban (cities) biomes
  • Biomes support ecosystems and biodiversity

Ahmedabad's Urban Biome & Sparrows

Sparrows once thrived in old Ahmedabad due to:

  • Open structures
  • Grain availability
  • Small gardens

Modern changes:

  • Sealed buildings
  • Pesticides
  • Fewer insects

Why It Matters

  • Their decline shows urban habitat loss
  • Conservation efforts: nest boxes, awareness, traditional designs

Research Foundation

Core Research Questions

What does it mean to be human‚Äîor post-human‚Äîwithin artificial, data-driven planetary ecosystems?

How can urban systems be extended or redesigned to adapt to non-Earth terrains such as Titan or Mars?

What kinds of bio-mimetic and machine-nature cohabitation systems could emerge from computationally derived planetary resources?

Project Overview

A speculative prototype for a game-based world-building system that envisions robot-driven planetary settlements, built on real-world datasets from extraterrestrial environments. It explores what forms of urban design, environmental response, and synthetic life might emerge in the absence of human-centered planning‚Äîwhile still maintaining human ethical concerns, memory, and agency.

Material Composition

Crafted from a mix of recycled plastics, bio-resins, and metallic finishes, these objects materialize the speculative infrastructures of an alien biosphere sustained by cyborg-nature hybrids.

Rhetorical Function

More than technical demonstrations, these pieces function as rhetorical devices, provoking reflection on humanity's evolving relationship with technology and the environment beyond Earth. They assert a representational position that challenges techno-solutionist narratives of space colonization by foregrounding symbiosis, fragility, and emergent complexity.

Situated Technology

This project aligns with CDP themes of critical technoscience and speculative realism. It operates as both a critique of current planetary data extractivism and a speculative tool for imagining alternative eco-technological futures.

Rhetorical Argument

Designing with planetary data is not neutral‚Äîit reflects and reproduces worldviews. This project argues that post-human ecosystems must be built ethically, not extractively. By turning robots into agents of bio-mimicry, not just efficiency, the project explores planetary coexistence without human supremacy.
